The Wasterwater-Climate Connection

How It Impacts Climate

  • Methane (CH‚ÇÑ): Produced when organic matter decomposes without oxygen. Methane is 25 times more potent than CO‚ÇÇ as a greenhouse gas.
  • Nitrous Oxide (N‚ÇÇO): Generated during nitrification and denitrification processes. Nearly 300 times more potent than CO‚ÇÇ.

When we fail to treat wastewater properly, these gases escape freely into the atmosphere, significantly contributing to climate change.

North American Context

  • United States: Approximately 97% of wastewater is treated, but aging infrastructure and rural gaps are issues.
  • Canada: About 69% treated, though remote areas face challenges.
  • Mexico: Only 64% receives proper treatment, leading to serious climate impacts.

These gaps in treatment directly contribute to greenhouse gas emissions, making wastewater an important but often overlooked factor in climate change.

What Can Be Done?

Improving wastewater treatment is a critical step in fighting climate change. Here are key recommendations for policymakers, communities, and individuals:

Upgrade Existing Infrastructure

Modernize treatment plants with energy-efficient technologies that capture methane for energy production.

Expand Treatment Coverage

Invest in expanding wastewater collection and treatment systems, especially in underserved areas.

Implement Resource Recovery

Adopt systems that recover resources from wastewater, including energy, nutrients, and clean water.

Monitor and Reduce Emissions

Install monitoring systems to track and reduce greenhouse gas emissions from treatment facilities.
